## What is the "Hollywood Dose"?
The "Hollywood Dose" refers to a low-dose protocol of Isotretinoin—typically just **10mg to 20mg taken once or twice a week**. 

Unlike the traditional high-dose course intended to cure acne permanently, the low-dose trend aims to:
- Permanently shrink sebaceous glands (minimizing pores).
- Eliminate all skin oiliness (the "glass skin" look).
- Reduce the frequency of minor hormonal breakouts.

While dermatologists have used low-dose protocols for years to treat persistent mild acne, the 2026 "Hollywood" trend has expanded to people with almost perfect skin who are seeking "filter-like" perfection.

## Safety Considerations in 2026
Despite the lower dosage, Isotretinoin remains a serious medication. Recent June 2026 safety data reminds us that even at a "Hollywood Dose," several risks remain:

1. **Teratogenicity:** The risk of severe birth defects remains identical, regardless of how low the dose is. Strict adherence to iPLEDGE (or similar programs) is mandatory.
2. **Liver & Lipid Impact:** Systemic absorption still requires regular blood work to monitor liver enzymes and cholesterol levels.
3. **The "Purge":** Even at low doses, many users experience an initial breakout and extreme dryness of the lips and eyes.
4. **Long-Term Unknowns:** There is limited data on the effects of staying on a "maintenance" dose of Accutane for years at a time.
