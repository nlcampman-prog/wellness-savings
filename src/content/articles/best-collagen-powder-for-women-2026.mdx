## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the difference between Type I, II, and III collagen?
*   **Type I:** Represents 90% of your body's collagen. It is highly concentrated in the skin, hair, nails, and bone structure.
*   **Type II:** Sourced primarily from chicken cartilage. It is the core building block of joint cartilage and spinal discs.
*   **Type III:** Found alongside Type I, primarily in muscles, internal organs, and blood vessels.
*   *For skin beauty and general joint health, a combination of **Type I and Type III** (like bovine collagen) is highly recommended.*
