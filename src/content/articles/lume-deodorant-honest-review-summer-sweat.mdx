## The Biochemistry of Body Odor: How Lume Actually Works

To understand why Lume is a massive departure from standard deodorants, we have to look at the microbiology of how body odor is created.

Many people believe that sweat itself is smelly. In reality, **eccrine and apocrine sweat is completely odorless**. 

Body odor is actually a byproduct of a biological chemical reaction. When you sweat, the natural bacteria living on your skin (specifically strains of *Corynebacterium* and *Staphylococcus*) feed on the lipids, proteins, and water in your sweat. 

As they metabolize these nutrients, they secrete volatile organic compounds (specifically short-chain fatty acids)—and *that* is what creates that distinct, pungent BO smell.

Traditional deodorants and antiperspirants address this in one of two ways:
1.  **Antiperspirants (Aluminum-Based):** Soluble aluminum salts physically plug your sweat ducts to stop moisture. However, many people prefer to avoid aluminum due to personal wellness choices or skin sensitivities.
2.  **Standard Deodorants:** Use heavy perfumes to cover up the smell, combined with antimicrobial agents (or high-pH baking soda) to neutralize bacteria.

```
Standard Deodorant:  Sweat + Skin Bacteria ===> Bacteria Eat Sweat ===> BO Smell ===> Cover with Perfume
Lume Acidified:      Sweat + Skin Bacteria + Lower pH (Mandelic Acid) ===> Bacteria Hibernate ===> ZERO Odor
```

### The Acidified Advantage (Mandelic Acid)
Lume takes a completely different, highly elegant approach. Rather than killing all skin bacteria or plugging your pores, Lume lowers the pH of your skin using **Mandelic Acid** (an alpha hydroxy acid derived from bitter almonds).

The bacteria that convert sweat into odor can only survive and function within a very specific, neutral-to-alkaline pH range. 

By gently lowering the skin’s pH, Lume creates an **acidified environment** that temporarily disables the bacteria's ability to feed on sweat. 

The bacteria essentially "hibernate" and are prevented from carrying out the chemical reaction that creates odor. 

Because Lume stops the chemical reaction at the source, you remain completely odorless. It doesn't mask odor; it prevents odor from ever being created in the first place.
