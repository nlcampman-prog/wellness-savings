## The Magnesium Trap: Oxide vs. Glycinate

The most common form of magnesium found in cheap supplements is **Magnesium Oxide**. Studies show that the absorption rate of magnesium oxide is as low as **4%**. The rest stays in your gut, drawing in water and acting as a laxative. 

If you want to sleep, you need magnesium that can actually reach your nervous system. That’s where **Magnesium Bisglycinate** (also known as Glycinate) comes in. It is bound to glycine, an amino acid that has its own calming effect on the brain.

## Dosage Guide: How Much Magnesium Do You Need?

The RDA for magnesium is generally between **310mg and 420mg** for adults, but many functional medicine practitioners recommend slightly higher amounts for those under high stress or who exercise intensely.

1.  **Start Low:** Begin with 100-200mg to see how your body reacts.
2.  **Timing:** Take your magnesium **30–60 minutes before bed**.
3.  **Watch the Gut:** If you experience loose stools, you are likely taking too much (or taking the wrong form, like oxide or citrate). Switch to **Bisglycinate** to avoid this.
