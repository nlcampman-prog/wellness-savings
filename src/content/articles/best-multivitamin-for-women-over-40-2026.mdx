## Key Nutritional Demands for Women Over 40

When choosing a multivitamin in your 40s, look for these critical, clinically backed ingredients:

1.  **Methylated B-Vitamins (Methylfolate & Methylcobalamin):** Up to 40% of women have a genetic mutation (MTHFR gene) that prevents them from converting synthetic folic acid and cyanocobalamin into active forms. Methylated B-vitamins are essential for energy, brain health, and managing anxiety or mood swings.
2.  **Vitamin D3 & K2 Co-Factor:** Calcium alone is not enough for bone density. Vitamin D3 absorbs calcium into your blood, while Vitamin K2 acts as the "GPS," directing that calcium out of your arteries and into your bones where it belongs.
3.  **Active Iron vs. No Iron:** Pre-menopausal women with heavy or irregular periods often require highly absorbable iron (like iron bisglycinate) to combat fatigue and anemia. However, post-menopausal women or those with normal iron levels should opt for an iron-free formula to prevent cellular oxidization.
4.  **Hormone & Stress Adaptogens:** Supporting the adrenal glands is paramount to easing the perimenopausal transition.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why do some multivitamins make me nauseous?
Nausea is typically caused by cheap, synthetic mineral salts (like zinc oxide or iron sulfate) dissolving too quickly in an empty stomach. To avoid this, always choose a premium multivitamin that uses **chelated minerals** (like zinc bisglycinate), and consume your vitamins with a healthy meal containing dietary fats.

### Do I still need a separate Vitamin D supplement?
Most standard multivitamins contain a bare-minimum dose of Vitamin D (often 400 IU). Premium over-40 multivitamins (like Needed) contain robust clinical doses (typically 2,000 to 4,000 IU), which usually eliminates the need for separate D3 supplementation, provided you get regular sun exposure.
