# Red Light Therapy 2026: Benefits, Science & Best Devices for Home Use

Red light therapy (also known as photobiomodulation) has exploded in popularity in 2026 — and for good reason. From celebrity skincare routines to professional sports recovery rooms, red light is being used for everything from anti-aging to muscle recovery.

## What Is Red Light Therapy?

Red light therapy uses specific wavelengths of red and near-infrared light to penetrate the skin and stimulate cellular energy production (ATP). This process, known as photobiomodulation, triggers a cascade of healing and regenerative effects throughout the body.

## The Science: How It Works

Red light (typically 630-660nm) and near-infrared light (810-850nm) penetrate the skin at different depths:

- **Red light (630-660nm)** — targets the skin surface for collagen production, wrinkle reduction, and wound healing
- **Near-infrared (810-850nm)** — penetrates deeper into muscles, joints, and tissues for recovery and pain relief

At the cellular level, these wavelengths stimulate the mitochondria to produce more ATP, which accelerates healing, reduces inflammation, and improves cellular function.
