## 5. Green Tea Extract (EGCG)

EGCG, the primary antioxidant in green tea, is a metabolic powerhouse. It inhibits the enzyme that breaks down norepinephrine, helping to mobilize fat.

- **Satiety Factor:** Studies suggest EGCG can slow the breakdown of GLP-1, keeping the hormone active in your system for longer.

## 6. Curcumin (Turmeric)

Beyond its anti-inflammatory benefits, Curcumin is an effective metabolic regulator. It helps to reduce the chronic low-grade inflammation that often blocks hormone signaling.

- **Hormone Balance:** Curcumin has been found to stimulate GLP-1 secretion and improve the function of pancreatic beta cells.

## 7. Psyllium Husk (Fiber)

Never underestimate the power of fermentable fiber. When fiber is broken down by your gut bacteria, it produces Short-Chain Fatty Acids (SCFAs) like butyrate.

- **The Butyrate Effect:** SCFAs are direct triggers for the release of GLP-1 from your intestinal cells.
- **Benefit:** It is the most cost-effective way to stay full longer and maintain a healthy gut environment.
