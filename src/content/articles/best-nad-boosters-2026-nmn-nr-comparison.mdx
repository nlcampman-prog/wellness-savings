## The NAD+ Precursor Showdown: NMN vs. NR

Since NAD+ itself is a large molecule that is difficult for the body to absorb directly via standard oral capsules, scientists focus on "precursors"—the building blocks your body uses to create NAD+.

### NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)
NMN has been the favorite of many longevity researchers, including Harvard's Dr. David Sinclair. 
*   **The Science:** NMN is one step away from becoming NAD+. In 2026, clinical trials continue to show its effectiveness in improving insulin sensitivity and muscle function in older adults.
*   **Pros:** Efficiently enters cells; supported by significant human data.
*   **Cons:** Regulatory status in some regions remains a topic of debate, though it is widely available in premium longevity blends.

### NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)
NR is perhaps the most commercially successful precursor.
*   **The Science:** NR must first be converted into NMN before it becomes NAD+. It has been extensively studied for its ability to increase NAD+ levels in the blood.
*   **Pros:** Generally Recognized as Safe (GRAS) by the FDA; high bioavailability.
*   **Cons:** Requires an extra enzymatic step compared to NMN.

## The 2026 Innovation: Liposomal NAD+

While precursors are great, **Liposomal delivery** has changed the game in 2026. By wrapping the NAD+ molecule (or its precursors) in a tiny bubble of fat (liposome), the supplement can bypass the harsh environment of the stomach and be delivered directly into the bloodstream.

*   **Why it works:** It mimics the body's own cell membranes, allowing for significantly higher absorption rates than standard powders.
*   **The Choice:** For those who want the most direct route to cellular energy, Liposomal NAD+ is often the top recommendation in 2026.
