## The Core Science: Amino Acid Utilization (AAU) Explained

To understand why Perfect Amino is a biological breakthrough, we must look at how the body processes different protein sources. 

The human body requires **20 amino acids** to function. Of these, 11 are non-essential, meaning our bodies can synthesize them internally. The remaining **8 are essential amino acids (EAAs)**:

1.  **L-Leucine:** Triggers muscle protein synthesis via the mTOR pathway.
2.  **L-Valine:** Supports muscle tissue repair and glucose metabolism.
3.  **L-Isoleucine:** Aids hemoglobin synthesis and muscle recovery.
4.  **L-Lysine:** Essential for collagen production, bone health, and antibody synthesis.
5.  **L-Phenylalanine:** A precursor for neurotransmitters like dopamine and norepinephrine.
6.  **L-Threonine:** Supports structural proteins like collagen and elastin.
7.  **L-Methionine:** Critical for metabolism, detoxification, and tissue growth.
8.  **L-Tryptophan:** Precursor for serotonin (mood stability) and melatonin (sleep architecture).

*Note: L-Histidine is sometimes classified as a ninth essential amino acid, but adult physiology can typically synthesize sufficient amounts from the other eight.*

For protein synthesis to occur, **all 8 EAAs must be present simultaneously and in a highly precise, proportional ratio**. If even one EAA is missing or under-represented, the entire chain halts. The remaining amino acids cannot be stored for later; they are immediately deaminated, producing toxic nitrogen byproducts (urea and ammonia) that must be filtered out by your kidneys.
