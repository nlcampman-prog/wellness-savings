## What is Sea Moss?

Sea moss, also known as Irish moss (*Chondrus crispus*), is a type of red algae found along the Atlantic coasts. It has been used for centuries in traditional cultures for respiratory and digestive health.

Its modern "superfood" status comes from its incredible nutrient density. Sea moss is famously cited for containing **92 of the 102 minerals** the human body needs to function, including iodine, magnesium, calcium, and potassium.

## The Weight Loss Connection: How It Actually Works

Sea moss is not a "magic pill" that melts fat overnight. However, it supports weight loss through several indirect biological mechanisms:

### 1. Thyroid Support (The Iodine Factor)
The most significant link between sea moss and weight loss is **iodine**. Your thyroid gland requires iodine to produce hormones that regulate your metabolism. If your thyroid is sluggish due to iodine deficiency, your body will struggle to burn calories efficiently. Sea moss provides a natural, food-based source of this essential mineral.

### 2. Satiety and Gut Health
Sea moss is rich in **carrageenan**, a type of soluble fiber. Like other fibers, it can slow down digestion and help you feel fuller for longer. Furthermore, it acts as a prebiotic, feeding the beneficial bacteria in your gut, which is increasingly linked to healthy weight management in 2026 research.

### 3. Nutrient Density vs. Cravings
Many cravings are actually the body’s way of signaling a nutrient deficiency. By flooding your system with 92 minerals, sea moss may help reduce "hunger signals" triggered by a lack of essential trace elements.

## Potential Side Effects and Safety

Because sea moss is so high in iodine, it is possible to get *too much* of a good thing. 
*   **Thyroid Disorders:** If you have an overactive thyroid (hyperthyroidism) or Hashimoto’s, consult your doctor before using sea moss.
*   **Heavy Metals:** Seaweeds can absorb heavy metals from the ocean. Always ensure your brand uses third-party testing to verify purity—a standard we've confirmed with Nume Health.
