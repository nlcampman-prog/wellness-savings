## Clinically Backed Benefits of Ashwagandha for Women

Ashwagandha (*Withania somnifera*), also known as Indian Ginseng, has been a cornerstone of Ayurvedic medicine for over 3,000 years. Modern clinical trials have validated its powerful effects, particularly for the female body:

### 1. Direct Cortisol & Stress Reduction
Cortisol is essential in acute "fight or flight" moments, but chronically high levels are destructive. 
*   **The Science:** Multiple double-blind, placebo-controlled studies have shown that high-purity Ashwagandha root extract can reduce serum cortisol levels by up to **22% to 30%** in stressed adults. This leads to a noticeable, calm, grounded feeling throughout the day.

### 2. Balancing Female Hormones & PCOS Support
Your thyroid, adrenals, and ovaries are connected via the **Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal (HPA) axis**. When your adrenals are constantly stressed, it signals your ovaries to down-regulate progesterone production, leading to "estrogen dominance."
*   **The Science:** By calming the adrenal glands and lowering cortisol, Ashwagandha helps restore the delicate progesterone-to-estrogen ratio. This can reduce severe PMS symptoms, regulate menstrual cycles, and support women struggling with Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS).

### 3. Deep, Restorative Sleep Quality
The translation of Ashwagandha’s botanical name, *somnifera*, literally means **"sleep-inducing."**
*   **The Science:** Active compounds called *withanolides* interact with GABA receptors in the brain—the same pathways targeted by anti-anxiety medications. This calms central nervous system activity, helping you fall asleep faster and stay in deep, restorative sleep phases longer without any morning grogginess.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take for Ashwagandha to work?
Unlike caffeine or pharmaceutical sleep aids, Ashwagandha is a cumulative herb. While some immediate calming benefits can be felt within 1 to 2 hours of taking Koi CBD or Goli, the robust endocrine and thyroid balancing benefits require **2 to 4 weeks of consistent daily use** to fully build up in your system.

### Does Ashwagandha affect thyroid function?
Yes. Ashwagandha is clinically shown to support a sluggish thyroid by increasing T4 (thyroid hormone) levels. If you suffer from hypothyroidism, it can be a fantastic natural support. However, if you suffer from hyperthyroidism (an overactive thyroid), consult with your endocrinologist before taking it.
