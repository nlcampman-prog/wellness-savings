## The Science Behind Weighted Comfort: Why It Works

If the idea of a weighted hoodie sounds gimmicky, the underlying science is actually well-established.

**Deep Pressure Stimulation (DPS)** — the application of firm, gentle, distributed pressure to the body — has been studied extensively in occupational therapy and mental health research. DPS activates the parasympathetic nervous system (the "rest and digest" branch), lowering heart rate, reducing cortisol, and increasing serotonin and dopamine levels.

This is the same mechanism that makes weighted blankets effective for anxiety and insomnia. A 2020 randomized controlled study published in the *Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine* found that participants using weighted blankets reported significantly reduced insomnia severity, better sleep maintenance, and decreased daytime fatigue compared to a control group.

Comfrt has simply taken this validated therapeutic concept and made it wearable. Instead of being confined to your bed under a 15-lb blanket, you can experience the calming effects of deep pressure while making dinner, working from home, or winding down on the couch.

For specific populations, this matters even more:

- **Postpartum mothers:** The postpartum period is characterized by dramatic hormonal shifts, sleep deprivation, and heightened stress sensitivity. Weighted comfort can be a non-pharmaceutical tool for managing postpartum anxiety and promoting the calm state needed for milk let-down during breastfeeding.
- **Highly sensitive people (HSPs):** Individuals with heightened sensory processing sensitivity often find deep pressure grounding and regulating in overstimulating environments.
- **Anyone recovering from burnout:** When your nervous system is stuck in sympathetic overdrive, gentle proprioceptive input (pressure) is one of the most reliable ways to signal "you are safe now."
