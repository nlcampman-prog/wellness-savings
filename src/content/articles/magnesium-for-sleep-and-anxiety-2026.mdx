## The Science: How Magnesium Calms the Brain and Body

Magnesium is the ultimate nervous system regulator. It targets anxiety and insomnia through three powerful biological pathways:

1.  **Regulating GABA:** Magnesium binds to and stimulates GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) receptors in the brain. GABA is an inhibitory neurotransmitter that acts as your brain’s natural "brake pedal," slowing down racing thoughts and signaling the mind to quiet down.
2.  **Reducing Cortisol:** Magnesium actively restricts the release of ACTH (adrenocorticotropic hormone), which prevents your adrenal glands from pumping excess cortisol (the stress hormone) into your bloodstream.
3.  **Relaxing Muscles:** Magnesium blocks calcium from entering muscle cells, allowing tight, tense muscles to physically relax. This is highly effective at preventing late-night muscle cramps and Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS).

## Glycinate vs. Citrate vs. Threonate: Which Type Do You Need?

Not all magnesium is created equal. The molecule that magnesium is bonded to completely changes how your body absorbs and utilizes the mineral.

*   **Magnesium Glycinate (Best for Sleep & Anxiety):** Bonded with the calming amino acid glycine. It is highly bioavailable, exceptionally gentle on the stomach, and is the absolute best form for relaxing the nervous system, reducing anxiety, and promoting deep sleep.
*   **Magnesium L-Threonate (Best for Brain Fog & Focus):** The only form of magnesium capable of crossing the blood-brain barrier. It is outstanding for improving cognitive function, memory, and focus, while also offering mild calming benefits.
*   **Magnesium Citrate (Best for Digestion):** Bonded with citric acid. It has a natural laxative effect, making it excellent for relieving occasional constipation, but it is not ideal for targeted sleep or anxiety support.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I take magnesium every day?
Yes. Magnesium is water-soluble, meaning your body absorbs what it needs and excretes any excess through urine. Daily supplementation of 200mg to 400mg is highly safe and beneficial for most adults, especially women. However, if you have chronic kidney disease, consult your physician before starting any mineral supplement.

### Does magnesium glycinate cause diarrhea?
Unlike magnesium citrate or oxide, which have strong osmotic laxative effects that pull water into the colon, **magnesium glycinate is highly absorbed by intestinal cells**. This means very little magnesium is left in the digestive tract, making it exceptionally gentle and virtually impossible to cause diarrhea or stomach cramping at standard doses.
