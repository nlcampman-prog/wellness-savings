### Sealing the Gut Barrier
Your gut lining is a incredibly thin, delicate single layer of cells held together by microscopic protein structures called "tight junctions." When these junctions are damaged by antibiotics, stress, pesticide residues, and poor diet, they become porous. 

This condition—known clinically as **increased intestinal permeability** (or "leaky gut")—allows toxins, undigested food particles, and pathogens to escape into your bloodstream. 

Your immune system immediately identifies these foreign invaders and triggers a state of chronic, systemic inflammation. This inflammation manifests directly as chronic bloating, food sensitivities, brain fog, fatigue, and skin flare-ups.

The **growth factors** in bovine colostrum act as a biological "sealant."
